What is a domain name?

A domain is an Internet address, where potential customers, business partners and friends can find you. It comprises of the domain name of your choice and the domain extension such as .pl, .eu, .com, .net, .co.uk, .fr and a whole lot of others. Just like there are no two street addresses that are exactly the same, domains are also unique in the whole Internet.

It is therefore important that a person who wants to visit your website, online shop or blog, is able to easily remember the address which they are supposed to type in the browser. It is best for the domain you choose to be obviously associated with you or your company, reflect the scope of your enterprise and correlate to the industry of you which you are a part.

In Poland, domains with the .pl extension are the most popular. You can also choose one of the functional suffixes, such as .com.pl or .shop.pl, or regional ones, such as .warszawa.pl. Globally, .com domains are the most popular, distantly followed by .net and .org. Throughout Europe, .eu has grown quite popular. It is also possible to own several domains with the same name and different extensions, such as premium.pl, premium.com, premium.eu.

Even if you don't plan to open your own company just yet, it might be a good idea to secure a good domain, because it can also be a good investment. If you end up not using the domain for your own ideas, it can grow more valuable over time and attract interest from potential buyers who can offer significantly more than what you originally paid.

Who needs a domain?

To mark your presence on the web and build your online image, you need a domain. It is necessary for big corporations and small businesses, news portals and bloggers, shops and institutions. By registering or purchasing your very own domain, you can become visible on the Internet. The website you establish on your domain is the gateway to your current and future users. Remember that over 5 billion people are connected to the Internet, including close to 30 million in Poland. With the proper use of your web address, you can reach millions!

To better promote your brand and enhance visibility of your company, it's best to secure a domain containing your business name.

If you specialise in an uncommon craft, try to buy a domain name describing your specific niche, for example millinery.com. If the industry is broad and you focus on some products, look for domains matching these products' names, like flip-flops.com.

Another way to build your web presence is looking for a domain that sounds original, metaphoric but can be associated with your business or industry. A catchy phrase such as "bells and whistles" for a design company or "abracadabra" for kids' shop corresponds with the website's content while also being easy to remember and promote.

Internet users often intuitively type an industry or product related address directly into the browser rather than use a search engine. I you own such a domain, you can be sure they visit your website first. Domains like that are the best investment.

How much should a domain cost?

Any product or service usually costs what the buyer is willing to pay fot it. It happens in retail, real estate, automotive, and it's also the case on the domain market. The investment in a domain is the investment in an address. The more prestigious the address, the more it usually costs. Depending on where you set up your business, customers might wander about searching for an obscure location, or they could easily find you right in the city center. That's why domains are often called virtual realties.

The price of a domain is highly dependent on its name and extension. English words in .com are the most valuable, with domains such as sex.com or property.com selling for over 10 million dollars. In Poland, .pl is the most popular and Polish words in .pl reach the highest prices, for example saturn.pl sold for 600.000 PLN and urlopy.pl (vacations) sold for 400.000 PLN. If the .pl domain of your choice is not available or is priced outside your budget, you can consider cheaper (for Polish words) alternatives, such as .com, .com.pl, .eu and others.

Not every good domain is priced at hundreds of thousands. In your search for the perfect name for a company or a product, you can find attractive domains priced way lower. For example blonnik.pl (fibre) was sold for 30.000 PLN and cukierki.pl (candy) for just 15.000 PLN.
